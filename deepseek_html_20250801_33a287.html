5.1 Alternative Investments

REITs: Real Estate for Teens

REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) let you invest in real estate without buying physical property. They trade like stocks!

Aspect REIT Physical Property Teen Viability
Minimum Investment $1-100/share 20% down payment ($40k+) REIT Accessible
Management Professional Self-managed REIT Better
Liquidity High (stock exchange) Low (6+ months sale) REIT Preferred
Dividends 4-8% annually Rental income REIT Consistent
Diversification Multiple properties Single property REIT Advantage

Case Study: $500 in VNQ REIT

Sarah (age 16) invested $500 from her babysitting savings in Vanguard Real Estate ETF (VNQ) in January 2021:

  • 2021 Investment: $500 @ $94/share = 5.32 shares
  • 2024 Value: $128.50/share × 5.32 shares = $685
  • Dividends Earned: $42 (average 3% yield)
  • Total Return: ($685 + $42) - $500 = $227 (45.4% return)

Key Insight: REITs offer affordable real estate exposure with liquidity teens need.

5.2 Retirement Accounts for Teens

Roth IRA: The Teen Superpower

Account Type Tax Treatment Withdrawal Age Teen Advantage
Roth IRA Tax-free growth 59.5+ Massive compounding potential
401(k) Tax-deferred 59.5+ Employer match (rare for teens)
Brokerage Capital gains tax Any age Flexible for college funds

The Magic of Early Roth IRA Investing

James (age 15) invests $3,000 from summer job earnings in a Roth IRA:

  • Investment: $3,000 in S&P 500 index fund
  • Annual Return: 7% (historical average)
  • Age 65 Value: $160,000 tax-free!

Roth IRA Growth Calculator

⚠️ Important Rules

  • Teens can contribute only up to their earned income ($7,000 max in 2024)
  • Parent/guardian must open a custodial Roth IRA
  • Early withdrawals may incur penalties except for education/first home

5.3 Investment Psychology

Behavioral Biases in Investing

Bias Effect Mitigation Strategy Teen Example
FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) Chasing trends Pre-set investment rules Buying crypto after 100% surge
Loss Aversion Holding losers too long Stop-loss orders Keeping falling meme stocks
Anchoring Fixating on past prices Regular fundamental reviews Refusing to sell stock at loss
Confirmation Bias Seeking confirming information Seek opposing viewpoints Only following bullish analysts

Meme Stock Case Study: AMC (2021-2024)

  • Jan 2021: $2/share
  • Jun 2021: $72/share (+3,500%)
  • Jan 2024: $4.50/share (-94% from peak)

Psychology Lesson: FOMO drove prices to unsustainable levels. Investors who bought at peak lost most of their money.
